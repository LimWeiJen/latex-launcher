\section{Body of Article}

This section contains the main body of the article. Equations within an article are numbered consecutively from the beginning of the article to the end.

\begin{equation}
    x = Y + Z. \label{eq:sample}
\end{equation}

Equation numbering should be consecutive, should appear flush right on line with the last line of an equation. 

\subsection{Figures and Tables}

All first citations of figures and tables in the article must be in numerical order. Citations to figures in text always carry the abbreviation ``Fig.'' followed by the figure number, for example, Fig.~\ref{fig:sample}.

\begin{figure}[tb]
    \centering
    % \includegraphics[width=0.45\textwidth]{images/placeholder.png}
    \caption{This is an example figure caption.}
    \label{fig:sample}
\end{figure}

The general style for table captions is such that each caption number should be centered above the table with the label TABLE and the enumeration given in Roman numerals.

\begin{table}[H]
    \caption{Table Example}
    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|}
            \hline
            \textbf{Header 1} & \textbf{Header 2} & \textbf{Header 3} \\
            \hline
            Row 1 Col 1 & Row 1 Col 2 & Row 1 Col 3 \\
            \hline
            Row 2 Col 1 & Row 2 Col 2 & Row 2 Col 3 \\
            \hline
        \end{tabular}
    \end{center}
    \label{tab:sample}
\end{table}

\subsection{Citations}

The numbering of references is employed by citing one reference per number, for example \cite{sample_ref}. Every reference in a Transactions reference list should be a separate number entry. Use of one reference number to designate a group of references is not permitted.
